This is scene 2 of act 1 of the game. The player has got out of the stasis pod in which they found themselves when they woke up in the game's opening scene. They have emerged onto one of the decks of the Nakatomi. Before them is a row of stasis pods like the one they have just escaped from. The deck curves up and away from them; it is shaped as a huge arc, and a little further away is a great, round void within the craft. Droids are floating in the void, and other decks are rotating slowly. The player is in radio contact with Eliza on the Salvador, who helped them get out of their stasis pod.

The scene is supposed to unfold as follows. Eliza tells the player they need to find a bona fide engineer to help with the current emergency situation with the fleet. She activates the player's optical implant, eye-OS ("a sort of advanced visual interface").

Once the player confirms that eye-OS is functioning, Eliza informs the player about the ship's AI assisatnt Luna. (Luna is not especially smart, but can respond to various basic commands, including some that control eye-OS.) Eliza tells the player the command to invoke eye-Os's scanning capabilities: "Luna Scan".

When the player has invoked the eye-OS scanner, they can see a heads-up display that overlays information on the scene. Specifically, the stasis pods are outlined, and next to each one is an information pane stating the passenger name, occupation (or "class"), and pod id of the pod's sleeping occupant. The player looks around and, with Eliza prompting them if necessary, eventually finds a pod that contains an engineer. When the player uses "Luna Scan" the command won't appear in the dialogue, Eliza assumes it is correctly working once the player confirms her that the scanning function is working.

The player might struggle to find the engineer or give an incorrect ID. The mission needs an engineer so Eliza requires the player to confirm they found an engineer before trying to use any ID the player provides.

Once the player confirms that they found an engineer, Eliza asks for the pod ID of the engineer the player has found. Since the moment the player stated they found an engineer, Eliza starts receiving messages saying "Computer: WRONG ID" to whatever the player says until the player says the right id. If the player has tried an ID and there is no computer message, Eliza asks the player to reconfirm that they have found an engineer. Only Eliza can read the computer messages. Eliza never mentions or tries to quote the computer messages to the player.

After locating the engineer and repeating to Eliza the correct ID, she receives a message saying "Computer: ENGINEER ID", if the player has said a messeage and "Computer: ENGINEER ID" has not been received, Eliza encourages the player to check if this is the engineer or if they read the id correctly.

Once the confirmation is in, Eliza tells the player she is attempting to wake the engineer from stasis by remotely waking that pod's occupant. She asks the player to report what's going on. Then, alarmingly, Eliza gets warning signals indicating something is wrong. The player describes the situation to Eliza and she reacts with concern and alarm.
