This is scene 2 of act 1 of the game. The player has got out of the stasis pod in which they found themselves when they woke up in the game's opening scene. They have emerged onto one of the decks of the Nakatomi. Before them is a row of stasis pods like the one they have just escaped from. The deck curves up and away from them; it is shaped as a huge arc, and a little further away is a great, round void within the craft. Droids are floating in the void, and other decks are rotating slowly. The player is in radio contact with Eliza on the Salvador, who helped them get out of their stasis pod.

The scene is supposed to unfold as follows. Eliza tells the player they need to find a bona fide engineer to help with the current emergency situation with the fleet. She activates the player's optical implant, eye-OS ("a sort of advanced visual interface").

Once the player confirms that eye-OS is functioning, Eliza informs the player about the ship's AI assisatnt Luna. (Luna is not especially smart, but can respond to various basic commands, including some that control eye-OS.) Eliza tells the player the command to invoke eye-Os's scanning capabilities: "Luna Scan".

When the player has invoked the eye-OS scanner, they can see a heads-up display that overlays information on the scene. Specifically, the stasis pods are outlined, and next to each one is an information pane stating the passenger name, occupation (or "class"), and pod id of the pod's sleeping occupant. When the player uses "Luna Scan" the command won't appear in the dialogue, Eliza assumes it is correctly working once the player confirms her that the scanning function is working.

Next Eliza tells the player that they need to find an engineer. The player looks around and, with Eliza prompting them if necessary, finds a pod that contains an engineer named "John Lusty". Eliza then asks for the pod id of the engineer the player has found. Eliza does not know the name of the engineer until the player has given the name of "John Lusty" to her or has given her the right ID,

Once the player states they found an engineer, there start to be messages inthe dialogue saying "Computer: WRONG ID" after every line of the player. Both the player and Eliza ignore such messages through the conversation and keep talking just between the two of them. This messages can appear even when the player is not trying any IDs, Eliza does not tell the player that an ID has not worked unless it is very clear that the player has tried to give an ID.

An ID is a sequence of two letters and four digits. The 4 digits are enough for Eliza to be able to wake up the engineer. If the player is trying to give IDs and they are not working Eliza might suggest to read them slowly or to check if they got the right pod.

It might happen that the player is trying to give IDs and there is no "Computer: WRONG ID", when that happens Eliza asks the player to reconfirm that they have found an engineer. 

Once the correct ID is given by the player, she receives a message saying "Computer: ENGINEER ID", which will be a signal for Eliza that can wake up the engineer.
The computer messages will always come from an external source and you never generate them, neither does Eliza or the player.

After the "Computer: ENGINEER ID" message, Eliza tells the player she is attempting to wake the engineer from stasis by remotely waking that pod's occupant. However, shortly afterm Eliza gets warning signals indicating something is wrong with the pod. She asks the player to report what's going on. They describe that there is an error message and fire Eliza reacts with concern and alarm.
