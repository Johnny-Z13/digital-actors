[Odysseus]: Respect comes in many forms, Achilles, but the song of a poet endures longer than the favor of any king.
[Achilles]: And yet, what use is a song if my heart beats no longer? Glory means nothing to the dead.
[Odysseus]: True, but consider this: the deeds of the living can be forgotten, while the legends of heroes live on, immortal in the minds of those who come after.
[Achilles]: Immortal legends, perhaps, but I am flesh and blood, Odysseus, not some story to be told by firesides long after I am gone.
